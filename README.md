### Project Overview

This project investigates appointment trends, regional variation and service efficiency within NHS healthcare delivery using multiple datasets. The analysis focuses on identifying demand patterns, evaluating workforce utilisation and assessing system performance across different regions and service types.

### Objectives
Identify seasonal and temporal trends in appointment demand
Analyse regional variation in healthcare service usage
Evaluate workforce distribution and service delivery patterns
Support data-driven improvements in healthcare planning and efficiency

### Key Findings
NHS services operate under high demand and utilisation, with increasing pressure over time
Care delivery is dominated by short, routine, face-to-face appointments
A shared workforce model is widely used, with growing reliance on non-GP staff
Significant regional variation exists in appointment volumes and service pressure
Data quality limitations impact the precision of insights

### Conclusions
The NHS appointment system reflects a high-demand environment, constrained capacity and increasing operational pressure. Workforce flexibility supports service delivery, inefficiencies remain due to uneven regional demand, missed appointments and limited adoption of digital consultation methods.
